Project Overview

Engineering Bone Material Onto a 3D Printing Platform

Tetranite is a regenerative bone adhesive that, when extruded as a binder onto bone-like powder, can build patient-specific structural scaffolds. Our team's job: take the OASIS open-source binder jet platform and adapt it to handle RevBio's material chemistry and process requirements.

I owned the Z-axis motion system, base plate, and powder containment subsystem.

Subsystem 01

Z-Axis Redesign & 2-DoF Compliant Coupling

The original Z-axis used precision-machined linear bearing mounts to keep the print head aligned through travel. This added cost, tight tolerance stack-ups, and a single failure point.

My approach: instead of demanding rigid perfection, design a mechanism that flexes just enough to absorb misalignment in the directions that don't matter — a 2-DoF compliant coupling with no bearings, no backlash, fewer parts, and zero cost of precision mounting.

Image · Original DesignOriginal Z-axis with precision-machined linear bearing mounts.
Image · 2-DoF Compliant CouplingCAD render of the compliant coupling replacement.
Why Compliant?

Flexure absorbs angular misalignment in two rotational DoF — exactly the failure mode precision mounts existed to prevent. No bearings to wear, zero backlash.

Outcome

Eliminated precision-machined mounts entirely. Significantly reduced cost and assembly complexity while improving Z-axis linear motion quality.

Subsystem 02

Piston Outer Sleeve Redesign

The original piston sleeve was designed without accounting for tolerance stack-up — it bound against the piston head during testing and hindered Z-axis linear motion.

I redesigned it as a split-half assembly: two FDM-printable halves that clamp around the piston with adjustable compression, converting a manufacturing constraint into a tunable parameter. Powder seal compression can now be dialed in post-assembly without disassembling the printer.

Subsystem 03

Piston Head & Drive Sizing

The original piston head's build volume and powder reservoir were too small for RevBio's print sizes. I ran length-to-diameter ratio calculations for piston stability under load, redesigned the piston head to expand capacity, and upgraded the drive system to a stronger stepper motor with a finer-pitch lead screw — giving greater torque multiplication and improved Z-resolution.
